# P19 — The Painted Journey (artist SVG lab)

**Try it:** `http://hasnains-mac-mini.local:8437/v3/?lab=19` (phone,
landscape). Drag in any direction to inspect the whole ground plane. The
camera eases home to the next journey spot. Add `&map=stub` to compare the
contract scaffold.

## What this is

The P18 terraced-valley idea rendered as the final programmable artist asset:
one 1800×860 SVG seen from a soft bird's-eye tilt, three daylight regions,
three geometric hearts, one walk, one stream, 21 future-proof spot anchors,
independent water layers, and a foreground occlusion layer. P19 parses that
contract and stages the game's living layer on top without reading or writing
the real save.

The simulation begins at `[5, 4, 0]` over occupied region counts `[5, 6, 6]`.
Tap the breathing star twice to finish the Orchard Rise and watch water climb
the second chadar into the Courtyard Heights. The extra SVG anchors remain
clear and invisible for future worlds.

## What to judge

1. **One found place:** does the aerial ground plane feel like a valley
   becoming an orchard becoming a high court, rather than three panels or a
   decorated progress row?
2. **Region identity:** can a child point to “the spring one,” “the fruit-tree
   one,” and “the stone one” without labels?
3. **The climb:** does the path visibly travel lower-left to upper-right while
   retaining enough switchbacks, depth, and breathing room to avoid a diagram?
4. **Living-layer contrast:** are the blooms, next star, Noor, child, daytime
   moon, and traveling light unmistakable against the painting?
5. **Occlusion:** does the bridge rail, orchard fringe, and courtyard arch
   make the child feel inside the landscape without hiding the route?
6. **Expansion seam:** at the upper-right, do path, water, terrain, and light
   clearly continue without a wall, peak, or finale?
7. **Phone window:** pan both axes at 852×393. Are the hearts and gates
   readable at actual size, with no important detail trapped at an edge?

## Keep / revise rule

Keep the asset if all three places read at phone size, the living layer owns
the attention, the camera can follow the ascent without exposing blank ground,
and the upper-right edge feels extendable. Revise the SVG—not the journey
mechanics—if a heart is ambiguous, a camera window feels empty/crowded, or any
static paint competes with an engine-drawn cue. Production title wiring waits
for this visual verdict.
