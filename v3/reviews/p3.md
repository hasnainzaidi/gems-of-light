# Prototype 3 — Ancient Ruins · Design Review

Played on: (tbd) · echo mode tested: off · sessions: (tbd)

1. **What emotion was this prototype optimized for?**

   Quiet awe and mystery. The level is built so the ruin *feels like it was
   once whole* — a processional colonnade leading inward, a plinth where a
   statue used to stand, a collapsed wall spilled into the grass, a half-buried
   chamber the temple sealed, a flooded cistern crossed on the heads of fallen
   stones, and an inner sanctuary opening onto a fountain courtyard. Nothing
   instructs the child; the broken geometry does. The question under test is
   whether that environmental mystery — "what were these stones guarding?" — is
   enough on its own to pull a child forward and make the gems feel like a
   discovery rather than a chore. The carved cream flagstone (tile 4) is the
   recurring tell that a builder's hand was once here, now half-swallowed by
   overgrowth.
