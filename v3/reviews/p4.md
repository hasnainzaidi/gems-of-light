# Prototype 4 — River Journey · Design Review

Played on: (tbd) · echo mode tested: (tbd) · sessions: (tbd)

1. **What emotion was this prototype optimized for?**

   Flow — the feeling of being gently carried. The signature raft mechanic
   replaces effortful traversal with steady, self-advancing motion: the child
   boards, and the current does the work while the surah is theirs to hold. The
   journey deliberately alternates being-carried (raft drift) with self-paced
   walking (banks, the plank bridge) so the calm never becomes passivity — each
   crossing arrives at a dry bank where the child steps off and chooses the next
   step. Falling into the water is a soft lift back to the bank, never a failure,
   so the river reads as wholly friendly. The open question this layout probes:
   does continuous forward movement soothe and pace the memorization, or does it
   remove enough agency that attention drifts with the current?
