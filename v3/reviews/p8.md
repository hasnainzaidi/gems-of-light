# Prototype 8 — landmark world · Design Review

Played on: iPhone-sized preview (852×393) · echo mode tested: off · sessions: (tbd)

1. **What emotion was this prototype optimized for?**

   Orientation — the quiet confidence of "I know where I am." A single
   enormous lighthouse-minaret stands at the exact centre of the level and
   rises past the top of the phone's view, so it is legible as a beacon from
   almost anywhere on the map. The path is laid out to *orbit* it: the child
   walks out along the low ground and passes the tower's foot; climbs the
   eastern step onto a high lane and passes it again at shoulder height; then
   drops back down to the tower's base, where the campfire and the shrine
   door wait at its very foot. Because the same fixture is seen three times
   from three heights, it becomes a mental anchor — a place the child can
   navigate *toward* and *around* rather than a corridor they are pushed
   down. The hypothesis under test: does one memorable central landmark make
   a level easier to hold in the head, and does that spatial memory support
   holding the ayat in the head too? Al-Falaq's five gems ring the beacon
   (two on the western approach, two on the mid rises, one high on the
   eastern lane), so every gem is remembered in relation to the tower.
