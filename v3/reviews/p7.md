# Prototype 7 — Storm to Sunrise · Design Review

Played on: (tbd) · echo mode tested: off · sessions: (tbd)

1. **What emotion was this prototype optimized for?**

   Relief through emotional contrast. The world opens dark and rain-veiled — a
   grey storm over deep night-blue (Al-Falaq's "Lord of the daybreak," sought
   as refuge from the dark). Each gem gathered thins the rain and drifts the
   palette toward the fullest radiant morning, so the child *feels* the storm
   pass and the sun rise as a direct reward for recall. The bet is that the
   arc from unease to warmth — the same arc as the surah's meaning — anchors
   the ayat more firmly than a uniformly pleasant setting would. The land
   physically rises eastward toward the campfire in the first clear light, so
   progress reads in the body as well as the sky.
