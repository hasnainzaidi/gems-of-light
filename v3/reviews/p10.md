# Prototype 10 — Guided Path · Design Review

Played on: (tbd) · echo mode tested: off · sessions: (tbd)

1. **What emotion was this prototype optimized for?**

   Serenity, and the specific calm of being led. The child never has to decide
   *where* — the noor-seed trail runs unbroken from start to gem 1 to gem 2 …
   to the campfire, arcing over the stream and up the slab stair exactly where
   the foot should land. Lanterns flank every gem like a doorway, so each ayah
   arrives inside its own small framed scene. All decision-making is spent on
   *moving forward* and *listening*, none on navigation. The five gems are set
   left-to-right in ayah order, so simply walking the corridor gathers Al-Falaq
   in sequence — the procession itself teaches the order.

   **Compare against p9 (Open World):** these two are the brief's deliberate
   pair. p9 asks "what if the child chooses the path?"; p10 asks "what if the
   child never has to?" The instrumentation split that matters is the
   wander-vs-beeline ratio and time-to-each-gem: p10 should show near-perfect
   beelines and low variance; p9 should show wandering and re-tracing. The open
   question this prototype exists to answer is whether being led feels *serene*
   (held, cinematic, pacing-driven) or *inert* (nothing to do but hold right) —
   and whether directedness helps or hurts recall of the ayah *order*.
